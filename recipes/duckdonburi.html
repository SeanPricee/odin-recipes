Duck Donburi

Description

Teriyaki Duck Donburi, a beautiful Japanese rice bowl featuring slow-cooked teriyaki duck leg served with a fried egg, rice & bok choi.

Ingredients

  • 2 Duck Legs
  • 125ml (1/2 Cop) Water
  • 2 Tbsp Light Soy Sauce
  • 1 Tbsp Dark Soy Sauce
  • 4 Tbsp Rice Wine
  • 2 Tbsp Dark Brown Sugar
  • 85g (1/2 Cup) Long Grain White Rice
  • 50 g (1 carrot around 10-15cm) Carrot
  • 2 Spring onions
  • 1 Large or 2 Small Bok Choi (about 250g total)
  • 1 Tbsp Sesame Seeds
  • 2 Eggs
  • Salt (For the Rice Water and Egg)
  • 1/2 tsp Sesame Oil

Steps

  1. Mix together the soy sauces, rice wine, water, and brown sugar.
  2. Score the skin of the duck breast and place it in an ovenproof dish.
  3. Pour the sauce mix over the duck and place in the oven at 180˚C or 350˚F for 90 minutes, basting the exposed duck with the sauce every 30 minutes.
  4. Remove the duck legs from the oven and set aside for 10-12 minutes under foil.
  5. Drain the fat from the cooking sauce and set aside.
  6. Have a pan of boiling water ready for when the duck is ready to remove.
  7. Season the water with a generous teaspoon of sea salt and add the rice.
  8. Cut the bok choi into quarters.
  9. Place the bok choi in the bowl dish that the duck legs have come out of, cover with foil.
  10. Turn the heat off on the oven and put the bok choi dish into it and allow it to sit for 10 minutes.
  11. Turn the heat down under the rice and simmer for 10 minutes or until the rice is slightly al dente, then drain through a fine-mesh sieve.
  12. Allow the rice to sit for a minute to steam dry a little then fork through to fluff up.
  13. Use a vegetable peeler to create ribbons of carrot.
  14. Slice the spring onions at 45 degree angle 2-3mm thick.
  15. Shred your duckand dress with some of the cooking liquid just before frying your eggs.
  16. Fry your eggs, how you prefer them, for me, it needs a crispy fringe and a runny yolk.
  17. Place the rice in the bottom of the bowl.
  18. Add the carrots followed by the duck
  19. Drain the bok choi and add then add the egg.
  20. Finally, sprinkle with sesame seeds, spring onion, and a drizzle of toasted sesame oil.
